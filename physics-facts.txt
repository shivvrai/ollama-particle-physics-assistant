1. The Standard Model of particle physics classifies all known elementary particles.
2. Quarks are elementary particles that combine to form hadrons, such as protons and neutrons.
3. There are six flavors of quarks: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom.
4. Leptons include electrons, muons, tau particles, and their associated neutrinos.
5. The Higgs boson is responsible for giving mass to other fundamental particles.
6. Photons are the gauge bosons that carry the electromagnetic force.
7. Gluons are the gauge bosons that carry the strong nuclear force, binding quarks together.
8. W and Z bosons are the gauge bosons that mediate the weak nuclear force, responsible for radioactive decay.
9. Neutrinos are extremely light, electrically neutral particles that rarely interact with normal matter.
10. Antimatter particles have the same mass as their corresponding matter particles but opposite charge.
11. When matter and antimatter collide, they annihilate each other, releasing pure energy.
12. The Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN is the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator.
13. Quantum chromodynamics (QCD) is the theory that describes the strong interaction between quarks and gluons.
14. A proton is composed of two up quarks and one down quark.
15. A neutron is composed of one up quark and two down quarks.
16. Dark matter makes up about 27% of the universe but does not interact with electromagnetic forces.
17. The top quark is the most massive of all observed elementary particles.
18. Neutrinos can oscillate between three different 'flavors' as they travel through space.
19. Muons are similar to electrons but are roughly 200 times more massive.
20. The weak force is the only fundamental interaction that violates parity-symmetry (C-symmetry).
